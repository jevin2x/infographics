Filipino Values in the
Digital Age

Traditional Values

Bayanihan

The spirit of community cooperation, where people help one another without expecting anything in return. It's about working together for the common good.

Modern Social
Media Values

Materialism

Owning trendy or branded items and showcasing them online. This can shift attention away from helping others toward personal status and wealth.

Malasakit

Malasakit means having genuine concern for others, often putting others' needs before your own. It reflects compassion and shared responsibility.

Self-reward & Individualism

Social media often promotes self-reward and individualism, encouraging people—especially the youth—to treat shopping and self-promotion as a way to feel good or gain attention, sometimes at the expense of empathy.

Paggagalang

Paggagalang, or respect, especially for elders and authority figures, is deeply ingrained in Filipino culture. It’s seen in how people speak, behave, and make decisions.

Personal Branding

The concept of personal branding is about building a curated version of yourself to attract followers or opportunities. While this can be empowering, it may sometimes encourage self-centered behavior and overlook humility and respect.

Kapwa

At the heart of many Filipino values is kapwa, the idea of shared identity and deep empathy for others. It promotes seeing others as equals and treating them with compassion.

Performance Identity

In the digital world, many users create a performance identity—a version of themselves designed to impress or entertain. This can lead to unrealistic standards and reduce genuine connection.
